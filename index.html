Introduction

Per FSAE EV.7, the Shutdown Circuit is a series loop where any of eight fault sources can open the loop and kill the tractive system. This project covers two of those segments — the Latchboard and BSPD circuit — designed and validated from scratch.

Form
Low-voltage self-latching relay PCB implementing three independent fault paths (BMS, IMD, BSPD) in the FSAE Shutdown Circuit series loop.
Function
Any fault opens and latches the corresponding relay stage. Car stays disabled until manual reset at the vehicle — driver cannot re-enable from cockpit (EV.7.2.3). BSPD monitors simultaneous brake + throttle, flagging sensor failure or stuck pedal.
Method
Three relay stages in series with the Shutdown loop. Each uses a self-holding NC contact. Comparator threshold tuning on BSPD line per EV.7.7.
Benefit
Isolates each fault source to a dedicated stage — no single-point failure. <50 ms response. Manual-reset requires no MCU logic.
Result
99% detection accuracy across 100+ bench tests. Passed FSAE electrical inspection. Integrated into competition vehicle HV safety chain.
